Hi all,

I've put together open-source test vectors for Level 1 (spreading codes) and would really appreciate cross-checks from other teams ahead of the workshop in June.

Repo: https://github.com/luar-space/lsis-afs-test-vectors

What it ships:

  - All 210 codes_prnNNN.hex in the format prescribed by the interoperability plan — Gold (2046 chips), Weil primary (10230), Weil tertiary (1500), and the four S0–S3 secondaries.
  - validate.py — a single-file, stdlib-only CLI: check-annex3, check-lans-afs-sim, diff, verify-manifest.
  - Two independent oracles bundled and re-verified in CI on every push:
      a. LNIS Vol A Annex 3 (normative) → 630/630 sections bit-exact
      b. LANS-AFS-SIM chip dumps (BSD-2-Clause, © Takuji Ebinuma) → 420/420 bit-exact
  - The competition interoperability.pdf and technical-faq.pdf are bundled as schema / errata references.

Thirty-second use:

  git clone https://github.com/luar-space/lsis-afs-test-vectors
  cd lsis-afs-test-vectors
  python validate.py diff /path/to/your/codes/

What would help us all: if your generator disagrees on any PRN or section, please open an issue on the repo. I'd much rather catch divergence here than at the interop bench in Cornwall — and a real disagreement is a win for everyone, regardless of which side is right.

This is the first of five planned drops — one per interop level. Frames (L2), I/Q signals (L3), cross-decode (L4), and message parsing (L5) will follow with the same two-oracle structure as we ship them.

Happy to chat here or as GitHub issues on the repo.
